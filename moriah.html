THE BIRTH OF CAMP MORIAH

CAMP MORIAH

For the Last one year (2012) MCO has been talking about the birth of a New baby : The Camp Moriah for which the program has had a long travail . Finally on the 29th Of January the program was birthed.A total of 26 students are proud to be the pilot participants of this program.

 

The camp is situated in Maseno which is West of Kenya within the Nyanza province and is housed by the FAME Children's Home in Maseno. The FAME home is managed by MCO.Camp Moriah discipleship program is a training program with a vision of "Raising a generation of responsible young people holistically equipped through the word of God".

 

The Camp serves a three fold goal of training, mentoring and commissioning o-level graduates from Mathare valley and other interested parties in a seven month empowerment program.The discipleship- mission training is based on the following key areas:
1. Discipleship training and personal management
2.Life skill training: modern farming methods, making beads, first Aid skills, basic cookery and basic garment making and repair
3.Basic bible/ theological courses
4.Leadership training
5.World missions and field experience

Curriculum

The program has six month training that equips the participants with relevant information in evangelism and bible truths. Field exposure in missions takes one month and is designed to give the participants firsthand experience and exposure to the least evangelized and underdeveloped communities. During the missions ,the participants are hosted by Kenyan missionaries working in various mission ground under different agencies. Currently, the program has 26 participants , 19 boys and 7 girls.Charges: To support one young person to the program, a total 20000 kshs. ( USD 250) is required. We appeal to our partners and friends to support a young person be part of this program.

 

Facts about Mathare

  • 70% of Nairobi's population of four million lives on 5% of the of the city's land area.
  • Mathare Valley contains a population greater than the cities of Seattle, Denver, or Boston, yet the slum covers an area of only three square miles. In comparison, Seattle covers 80 square miles, Boston 42 square miles, and Denver 150 square miles.
  • It is estimated that one of every three adults in Mathare is HIV positive.
  • The average life expectancy for a person who is HIV positive in Mathare is five years or less.
  • Common health problems for children in Mathare include dysentery, malnutrition, malaria, typhoid, cholera, infections, tetanus, and polio.
  • Juvenile heads of households are common. This is a situation where a child or teen, some as young as 8 or 9 years old, is left to care for younger siblings, since both parents have died of AIDS.
  • Mathare Valley has no police or fire protection, and no paved roads. The police in Nairobi are not allowed to enter the slum.
  • Without an education the children in the Mathare Valley often turn to a future of crime, prostitution, drug abuse and disease.
  • There are three government spublic schools and one youth polytechnic in Mathare. A number of individuals and agencies run schools (kindergarten and primary), a number of which are classified as non-formal schools.

  • Mathare valley is a collection of 3 villages – No. 10, Mathare 4A and Mathare 4B – with an estimated population of 750,000

  • HIV/AIDS is amajor issue with high infection rates in the area.

  • Over 90% of the household are headed by single women, many of whom have been in abusive relationships.
